Game Design Document: Lambda's Labyrinth

Core Concept

"Lambda's Labyrinth" is a narrative-driven puzzle-adventure game where players embody Cam Arlen, a disillusioned computer linguist. Through mysterious circumstances, Cam stumbles upon a hidden, functional layer of reality governed by the principles of Lambda Calculus and Combinatory Logic. Players learn these advanced computer science concepts by manipulating the game world through interactive puzzles, as Cam's perception of reality shifts and expands. The game explores profound themes of identity, choice, and the nature of abstraction, culminating in a climactic decision that redefines Cam's existence.

Target Audience & Genre

  • Primary Audience: Players interested in logical puzzles, narrative-rich indie games, and philosophical themes.
  • Secondary Audience: Computer science students, educators, and professionals seeking an engaging learning tool.
  • Genre: Puzzle-Adventure, Narrative-Driven, Serious Game.
  • Age Rating: Teen+ (due to philosophical complexity).

Setting & Timeline

The game is set in a late 2000s to early 2010s contemporary world. This period allows for the technology (USB drives, basic HTML, command-line interfaces, older monitor types) to feel grounded and relatable as "mundane," providing a strong contrast to the unfolding "magical" reality of functional logic.

Key Themes

  • Abstraction & Reality: Is fundamental reality built from functions/logic?
  • Identity: Cam's journey of self-discovery and questioning existence.
  • Choice & Consequence: How logical decisions impact narrative outcomes.
  • Order vs. Chaos: The conflict between structured logic and unbounded paradoxes.
  • The Mundane Made Magical: The shift from corporate life to logical awakening.
  • Knowledge & Power: The ability to manipulate reality through code.

Gameplay & Mechanics

Core Game Mechanics

  • Interactive Lambda Puzzle System: Visual representation of lambda expressions as interactive blocks or nodes. Player directly manipulates elements to perform reductions, which is the primary method of progression.
  • Environmental Transformation: The game world dynamically changes to reflect Cam's deepening understanding. From subtle glitches to overt code manifestation and reality distortion.
  • Narrative Choice Interface: Key narrative decisions are presented as complex functional expressions that the player must correctly reduce to achieve their desired outcome.
  • In-Game Journal/Lore System: Provides optional context, deeper theory, and objective tracking for players.

Transition Strategies (Linking Scenes)

  • Crystal Clear Objectives: Each scene ends with a clear narrative prompt (dialogue, journal update) indicating the next goal.
  • Purposeful Transitions: Using short cinematic cutscenes, playable environmental walkthroughs, and interactive mini-puzzles to bridge scenes.
  • Pacing & Emotional Arc: Alternating between puzzle-heavy and narrative/exploration-focused scenes to prevent fatigue.
  • Consistent Visual & Auditory Language: The art style and soundscape evolve with the narrative to maintain immersion.

Core Learning Objectives

  • 1. Identity Function ($\lambda x.x$)
  • 2. Function Application
  • 3. Beta-Reduction (Substitution)
  • 4. Conceptual Abstraction
  • 5. Currying
  • 6. Booleans (True/False as functions)
  • 7. Church Numerals & Arithmetic
  • 8. Alpha-Renaming (Variable Equivalence)
  • 9. Variable Scope
  • 10. If/Else Logic using Lambda Expressions
  • 11. SKI Calculus (Variable-Free Logic)
  • 12. Recursion & Fixpoints (Y Combinator)
  • 13. Non-Termination & Infinite Recursion ($\Omega$)
  • 14. Self-Application
  • 15. Combinatory completeness
